Is SWEAT a measure of how hard you are working out?

The answer is NO!Â 
Body creates sweat to cool you off when body temperature gets too high.If anything, it's an indicator of how hot your body is

You're born with 2 to 4 million sweat glands. How much you sweat depends on the number of glands you have. Sweat amount is affected by how hot it is, intensity of workout, whether you smoke, drink coffee or cocktails -all of these increase perspiration.

Fat acts an insulator so overweight people sweat more.

As your body becomes more conditioned it takes more intense exercise to increase core body temperature and produce sweat. This doesn't imply if you sweat often, you are necessarily unfit. Other major reasons of no sweat are LESSER number of sweat glands or DEHYDRATION.

So if you are working crazy and hardly sweating, you might be super fit, blessed with minimal sweat glands or dehydrated.

So never use amount of sweat=calories burned.

What matters is intensity of your training.
