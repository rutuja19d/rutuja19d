Who made your clothes & why is that important?

Here are some statistics that will blow your mind-
-The fashion industry is the second largest polluter in the world, after oil.
-Fashion Industry contributes 10% of global greenhouse gas emissions due to its long supply chains and energy intensive production.
-Nearly 20% of global waste water is produced by the fashion industry 20,000 litres is the amount of water needed to produce one kilogram of cotton; equivalent to a single t-shirt and pair of jeans. (Using recycled cotton saves 20,000 litres of water per kilogram of cotton, a water-intensive crop.)
-It takes more than 5,000 gallons of water to manufacture just a T-shirt and a pair of jeans (that’s more than 18927 litres of water for one ‘basic’ outfit!)
-A few communities have textile recycling programs, about 85% of this waste goes to landfills where it occupies about 5% of landfill space and the amount is growing.
-Up to 95% of the textiles that are land filled each year could be recycled

*The fashion industry emits more carbon than international flights and maritime shipping combined*. READ THAT AGAIN.

Not to mention the modern slavery, working conditions and living wages of garnet workers in developing countries, where fast fashion brands manufacture their clothes and pollute our waters with toxic chemicals. I read a New York Times article the other day. A lot of websites give statistics and highlight issues of modern slavery, but this NY times article has published some personal stories of a few garment workers around the world and their struggles.

These stories, these faces, and so many more, along with the environmental footprint of the fashion industry-is exactly why you should care about who made your clothes.

There are so many upcoming brands that are addressing these problems in their own capacity. Unless we ask the right questions, we won’t find them.
