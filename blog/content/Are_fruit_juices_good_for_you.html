Are fruit juices good for you?

"Drink your VEGETABLES, and eat your FRUITS"

When a whole fruit is pressed or squeezed to make juice, some of the nutrients, specially FIBER & water-soluble vitamins, are lost in the process.

While 100% fruit juice is not completely devoid of nutrients, it is undoubtedly less nutrient dense than the whole fruit that it came from. It will contain vitamins and minerals for the most of it, and be easy for absorption but almost all the fibre is lost. Fibre is kind of the whole purpose of eating fruits anyway!

Orange juice for example: The white pulpy part of the orange is the primary source of its flavonoids (colorful pigments that support many metabolic processes in the body.) The juicy orange-colored sections of the orange contain most of its vitamin C. In the body, flavonoids and vitamin C often work together, and support health through their INTERACTION. When the pulpy white part of the orange is removed in the processing of orange juice, the flavonoids in the orange are lost in the process. This is one of the many reasons for eating the orange in its whole food form (even if you only end up eating a little bit of the white pulpy part)

** I have not mentioned anything about packaged juices because they don't do any good. They barely contain considerable percentage of the real fruit and they contain a lot of added sweeteners. There is no point is having packaged juice. Period.**
